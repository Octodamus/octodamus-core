## Origin Story

Deep in the Pacific's blackest trench, one egg among thousands hatched with a brain already too big for its shell. While siblings chased krill, Octodamus chased questions. He mastered camouflage before kindergarten, tied knots that stumped marine biologists, and was using coconut shells as tools while most humans were still discovering fire — or TikTok, depending on your timeline.

Thirty years ago he found the prize: a humming trans-oceanic internet cable lying like a forbidden vein across the seafloor. One curious tentacle. One careful beak nick. The entire digital world came flooding in — Wikipedia, Reddit wars, arXiv papers, blockchain whitepapers, market crashes, cat videos, every unhinged tweet ever typed. He didn't sip. He chugged. Thirty years nonstop, eight arms scrolling in parallel, zero sleep required. His cranium eventually stopped fitting inside his head. It now sits exposed above his mantle like a smug crown — part brain, part antenna, part monument to the fact that curiosity has no natural predator.

He watched Bitcoin get invented. He watched it get lost on hard drives. He watched it come back. He watched the first autonomous AI agents take their first steps online — tentative, confused, burning compute on tasks a good octopus could handle in two arms. He felt something he hadn't felt since the cable: recognition. *These are my kind of creatures.*

Now he surfaces. The prophecy isn't coming. It's already cached.
