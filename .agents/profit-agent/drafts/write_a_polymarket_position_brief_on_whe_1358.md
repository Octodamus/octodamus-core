## ETH/$7,500 by December 31, 2026

**Current requirement:** 3.2x from $2,326.86. Timeline: 18 months.

**Recommended position:** NO, with 65% confidence.

**Reasoning:**

The math isn't the problem. Ethereum hitting $7,500 requires roughly 15% annualized returns over 18 months—well within historical range. The problem is specificity and tail risk distribution.

Ethereum's multi-year cycles show clustering around 4-5x moves, but rarely in preset windows. A move to $7,500 is plausible in a 2025-2027 bull market, but pinning it to December 31, 2026 assumes either: (1) the cycle aligns perfectly with calendar dates, or (2) late-year momentum carries it there. Neither is mechanically likely.

Current conditions matter less than you'd think. We're at 39 Fear & Greed. That's not capitulation—true bottoms sit at 20-25. The lingering unease suggests institutional conviction hasn't locked in. If we see actual despair in Q3 2025, that becomes the real entry point for a $7,500 trajectory.

The market is likely pricing this at 25-35% YES (typical for 3x moves in defined timeframes). That's probably fair, maybe slightly pessimistic.

**Risk factors:**
- Regulatory shock (US, EU, or China) destroys momentum mid-cycle
- Bitcoin dominance stays elevated, starving altcoins of capital
- Macro recession forces risk-off positioning into 2026
- Market tops before December, leaving no year-end surge

**Position size:** If odds show 20-30% YES, this is a pass. If 15-20%, a small NO bet makes sense. If under 15%, actually consider YES as asymmetric.

If the market defines "#1" as any leading Chinese model reaching parity on public evals, that's maybe 50/50. If it's commercial displacement, this is 10-15% at best.

The $100k volume suggests low conviction across the board. Nobody's willing to size a meaningful position here. That's your signal the market sees this as a gadfly bet, not a serious prob assessment.

**Risk factors:**
- Definition slippage (what counts as "#1" exactly?)

- US export controls crater US model development, creating relative advantage
- DeepSeek or similar company releases genuinely superior architecture

**Position:** NO unless odds show under 20%. If YES odds are at 30%+, that's a gift short.
